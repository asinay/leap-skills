# positioning-builder
**Define your unique professional value in one clear paragraph — so you always know how to introduce yourself and why someone should choose you.**

Most executives can talk for 20 minutes about their career and still leave someone unsure what they actually do, what makes them different, or why it matters. This skill fixes that.

---

## The Prompt

```
Read my voice-builder and audience-builder profiles if they exist in this project.

I want to define my professional positioning — the clear, compelling answer to "what do you do and why does it matter?" that I can use everywhere: LinkedIn, bio, speaking intro, job interviews, networking conversations.

Ask me these questions one at a time:

1. What is the specific transformation you create for people or organizations? Not what you do — what changes because you showed up?
2. Who specifically do you create that transformation for? (Title, industry, situation — not "everyone")
3. What's your method or approach that others in your field don't use? What makes how you do it different?
4. What do you believe about your field that drives everything you do — your core professional conviction?
5. What's the evidence that your approach works? Give me 2-3 specific results (numbers, outcomes, transformations).
6. What do you want to be known for in 3 years that you're not fully known for yet?

After I answer, create my Positioning Suite:

**ONE-LINE POSITIONING STATEMENT**
"I help [specific who] [achieve specific outcome] through [unique approach]."
Write 3 versions — punchy, credible, and human. I'll pick one.

**THE 30-SECOND INTRODUCTION**
What I say when someone asks "what do you do?" at an event. Conversational, not rehearsed-sounding. Ends with something that makes them ask a follow-up question.

**THE TWO-MINUTE VERSION**
For interviews, speaking introductions, or podcast guest bios. Story arc: where I came from → what I do now → why it matters → what I'm working on.

**THE WRITTEN BIO (third person, 150 words)**
For conference programs, websites, and introductions. Strong, specific, no filler adjectives.

**WHAT I AM NOT**
3 things to explicitly exclude from how people describe me — to prevent being mis-positioned or misunderstood.

Save this as my positioning reference for all future content and outreach work.
```

---

## When to re-run this skill

- When you change roles or industries
- When you start a new business or pivot an existing one
- When your positioning no longer feels true
- Before a major job search or speaking campaign
- Any time someone describes you in a way that doesn't feel right

**Your positioning is a living document, not a permanent label.**
