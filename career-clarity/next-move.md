# next-move
**Decide with clarity what your next career move actually is — before you start applying, networking, or updating your resume.**

Most career mistakes aren't execution problems. They're clarity problems. This skill forces the honest conversation most people avoid having — with themselves.

---

## The Prompt

```
I'm at a career crossroads and I need to think through my options with clarity — not just optimism or fear.

Ask me these questions one at a time. Don't move to the next until I've answered:

1. What are the realistic options in front of you right now? List every path you're actually considering — even the ones that feel too scary or too small.
2. For each option: what does your gut say? (Not what's logical — what do you actually want?)
3. What would you do if money wasn't a factor for the next 2 years?
4. What would you do if you knew you couldn't fail?
5. What are you most afraid of about each option?
6. Who's depending on you, and how does that shape this decision?
7. What does "success" look like in 3 years for each path — specifically, not abstractly?
8. What's the cost of NOT deciding? What happens if you stay exactly where you are for another 12 months?

After I answer, give me:

**OPTION ANALYSIS**
For each path I listed: the real upside, the real risk, and the thing I'm probably not admitting to myself about it.

**THE HONEST ASSESSMENT**
Based on everything I've shared — what does the pattern of my answers suggest about what I actually want?

**THE DECISION FRAMEWORK**
3 criteria that matter most for MY specific situation (not generic advice). A simple way to evaluate any option against those criteria.

**THE NEXT 30 DAYS**
If I chose the path that my honest answers point toward — what are the first 5 concrete actions? Not "explore options" — actual steps with actual outputs.

**THE QUESTION I NEED TO SIT WITH**
One question that cuts to the core of what's actually holding me back. Something to think about before I decide.

Note: Be direct with me. Don't tell me what I want to hear. I need clarity more than comfort.
```

---

## This skill works best when you're honest

Claude will reflect back what you actually say — not what you meant to say. The quality of this conversation depends entirely on your willingness to be honest in your answers, especially to questions 3, 4, 5, and 8.

Most people already know what they want. They just haven't given themselves permission to say it out loud yet.
