# pivot-mapper
**Map your transferable skills to a new role, industry, or chapter — and build the bridge from where you are to where you want to go.**

Career pivots fail most often not from lack of skill — but from lack of a clear narrative connecting the past to the future. This skill builds that bridge.

---

## The Prompt

```
Read my voice-builder profile if it exists in this project.

I'm considering a career pivot and I need to think through it clearly — what's transferable, what's missing, and how to position myself for where I want to go.

Ask me these questions one at a time:

1. Where are you now? (Current role, industry, level — what your day actually looks like)
2. Where do you want to go? (Be as specific as you can — role, industry, type of company, seniority level, even specific companies if you have them)
3. What's driving this? (Be honest — what are you moving toward, and what are you moving away from?)
4. What do you think is stopping you or making this feel hard?
5. List every skill you use regularly in your current role — hard skills, soft skills, leadership skills, all of it.
6. What have you done outside your official job title that's relevant? (Side projects, volunteer work, advisory roles, content, speaking, etc.)

After I answer, produce:

**TRANSFERABLE SKILL MAP**
A clear list of my existing skills organized by how directly they transfer to my target role:
- Direct transfers (same skill, different context)
- Adjacent transfers (similar skill that needs reframing)
- Hidden transfers (experience that's more relevant than it looks)

**THE GAP ANALYSIS**
What's actually missing between where I am and where I want to go — and an honest assessment of how significant each gap is (critical / moderate / minor).

**THE GAP PLAN**
For each critical gap: a specific, realistic way to close it in 90 days or less. Certifications, projects, connections, writing, volunteering — whatever makes sense for my situation.

**THE PIVOT NARRATIVE**
A 3-sentence bridge that connects my past to my future in a way that makes sense to a hiring manager or decision-maker — not "I want a change" but "here's why my background makes me exactly right for this."

**QUICK WINS**
3 things I can do this week to start making the pivot real — not just planning it.
```

---

## A note on pivots

The biggest mistake people make in a career pivot is waiting until everything is perfect before starting.

The second biggest mistake is restarting from zero when you have 10-20 years of compound experience that transfers more than you think.

This skill is designed to help you see both clearly.
