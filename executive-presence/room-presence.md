# room-presence
**Prepare for any high-stakes meeting, presentation, or conversation so you walk in as the most prepared person in the room.**

Executive presence isn't a personality trait — it's preparation made invisible. This skill is your pre-meeting briefing system.

---

## The Prompt

```
Read my voice-builder profile if it exists in this project.

I have an important [meeting / presentation / conversation / interview] coming up and I need to walk in fully prepared.

Ask me these questions to understand the situation:

1. What is this meeting/event, and who will be in the room?
2. What is the official purpose — and what do you think the real purpose is?
3. What outcome do YOU want from this conversation?
4. What outcome do THEY want?
5. What's the one thing you're most worried about — a tough question, a difficult person, a topic you're not confident on?
6. What do you know about the key decision-makers in the room? (Background, priorities, communication style, past interactions)
7. What's your current relationship with the most important person who will be there?

After I answer, prepare the following:

**Situation Brief:** What's actually happening here (2 sentences — honest read)
**My Goal:** Stated clearly, not diplomatically
**Their Goal:** What they're walking in wanting
**The Tension:** Where those goals may conflict and how to navigate it
**3 Things I Must Communicate:** Non-negotiables I need to land before I leave the room
**Anticipated Hard Questions:** 3-5 questions I might get, with a strong answer for each
**My Opening:** How I should start — the first 30 seconds
**My Closing:** How I should end — what I want the last thing they hear to be
**If It Goes Sideways:** One tactic for each of these scenarios — the room goes quiet, someone challenges me, the conversation goes off track

Format this as a clean one-page brief I can review the night before.
```

---

## Best used for

- Board presentations
- Performance reviews (giving or receiving)
- Executive interviews
- Difficult conversations with a boss, peer, or direct report
- Salary negotiations
- First meetings with a C-suite stakeholder
- Pitching a new idea or budget request

**Run this 24-48 hours before the event.** Read the brief the morning of.
