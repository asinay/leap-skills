# thought-leader-post
**Share your expertise publicly without feeling like you're bragging, lecturing, or performing.**

The biggest block executives have with content isn't writing ability — it's permission. This skill removes the friction between what you know and what you share, and turns your real opinions into posts people actually want to read.

---

## The Prompt

```
Read my voice-builder and audience-builder profiles if they exist in this project.

I want to write a LinkedIn post that shares a real perspective from my career — something I genuinely believe, have learned, or have seen that others in my field should hear.

Ask me these questions one at a time:

1. What's something you believe about [your field / leadership / careers] that most people in your industry would push back on?
2. What experience taught you that — give me the specific moment, not the abstract lesson.
3. Who is this really for? Who needs to hear this the most right now?
4. What do you want them to feel after reading it? (Validated? Challenged? Inspired? Seen?)
5. What's the one thing you want them to walk away thinking or doing differently?

After I answer, write a LinkedIn post that:
- Opens with a hook that earns the "see more" click (not a question, not a cliché)
- Uses my real story or moment as the proof — not hypotheticals
- States my actual opinion clearly — no hedging, no "it depends"
- Ends with a question or CTA that invites real conversation
- Sounds like me, not like a LinkedIn influencer
- Stays between 150-300 words

Then give me:
- 2 alternative hooks I could use instead
- A one-sentence version for the first comment if I want to add context
- 3 hashtags that fit

Do not use: em dashes, bullet lists of three parallel things, "game-changer," "dive deep," "at the end of the day," or any phrase that sounds like it came from a content template.
```

---

## What makes a thought leader post work

The formula is always the same:
**Strong opinion + Real story that proves it + Specific audience it's for**

The posts that build real audiences aren't the ones with the most useful tips.
They're the ones where someone reads the first line and thinks: *"Finally, someone said it."*

**Run this weekly.** One real opinion, told through one real story, to one real person — every week.
