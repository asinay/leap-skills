# voice-builder
**Read this first. Every other skill in this library uses it.**

Your voice is the difference between content that sounds like everyone else and content that sounds like you. This skill extracts your authentic professional voice so Claude can write *as* you — not as a generic LinkedIn expert.

---

## How to use this skill

Copy everything below into a new Claude Project. Name the project something like "My Career Content."

Then paste this prompt:

---

## The Prompt

```
You are helping me build a personal voice profile so you can write content that sounds authentically like me.

Ask me the following questions one at a time. Wait for my answer before moving to the next. After I answer all of them, write a Voice Profile I can save and use in all future conversations.

Questions:
1. Describe your career in 3 sentences — the way you'd explain it at a dinner party, not on a resume.
2. What's the one thing you believe about careers or leadership that most people get wrong?
3. Paste 2-3 examples of writing you've done — emails, posts, notes, anything. (If you don't have any, describe how you naturally speak: formal or casual? Direct or warm? Short or detailed?)
4. Who is the one person you're always writing for — the version of yourself 5 years ago, a specific colleague, a type of leader? Describe them.
5. What topics are you most confident talking about? What topics make you hesitant?
6. What do you never want to sound like? (E.g., "corporate," "preachy," "salesy," "academic")

After I answer all 6 questions, create a Voice Profile with these sections:
- Tone & Personality
- Vocabulary & Phrasing Patterns
- What I Avoid
- My Core Belief (the through-line in everything I write)
- Sample sentence starters that sound like me

Save this profile and reference it every time you help me write anything.
```

---

## What to do with the output

Once Claude generates your Voice Profile:
1. Copy it
2. Create a new file in your Claude Project called `my-voice.md`
3. Paste it there
4. Every other skill in this library will automatically read it

**This is your foundation. Do this before anything else.**
