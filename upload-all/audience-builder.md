# audience-builder
**Run this after voice-builder. Defines who you're writing for so every skill hits the right person.**

Most content fails not because of bad writing — but because it's written for everyone, which means it resonates with no one. This skill builds your Audience Profile: the specific person you're always speaking to.

---

## The Prompt

```
I need to build a clear picture of my core audience so my content, outreach, and messaging always speaks directly to them.

Ask me these questions one at a time. After I answer all of them, create an Audience Profile I can reference in all future content work.

Questions:
1. Who is the specific person you most want to reach? (Title, industry, stage of career — be as specific as possible)
2. What is the #1 thing keeping them stuck right now?
3. What have they already tried that hasn't worked?
4. What do they secretly want but feel embarrassed or afraid to say out loud?
5. What does success look like for them in 12 months?
6. Where do they spend time online? What content do they already consume?
7. What words or phrases do THEY use to describe their own situation? (Not what you'd call it — what they'd say)

After I answer, create an Audience Profile with:
- Who They Are (1 paragraph)
- Their Core Frustration (the real one, not the surface one)
- Their Secret Desire
- Words They Use (a list of phrases to mirror back in content)
- Content That Resonates vs. Content That Repels
- One sentence I can use as a filter: "This piece of content is for [person] who [situation]."

Save this profile alongside my voice profile.
```

---

## Why this matters

Every skill in this library will write better content when Claude knows:
- Who you're speaking to
- What language they use
- What they actually need to hear

The audience profile turns generic advice into content that makes someone think *"this was written for me."*
