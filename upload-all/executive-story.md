# executive-story
**Turn your career experience into the stories that make rooms remember you.**

Most executives undersell themselves — not from lack of achievement, but from lack of story. This skill extracts your 5 most powerful career stories and structures them so you can use them in interviews, board rooms, keynotes, and content.

---

## The Prompt

```
Read my voice-builder profile and audience-builder profile if they exist in this project.

You're going to help me build a personal story bank — 5 career stories I can use in any high-stakes situation.

First, ask me these questions to gather raw material. One at a time:

1. Tell me about a moment in your career where something went seriously wrong and you had to figure it out. What happened, what did you do, and what was the result?
2. Tell me about a time you made a decision that felt risky at the time. What drove you to make it? How did it turn out?
3. What's the biggest transformation you've led — in a team, organization, or someone else's career?
4. What's a moment where you failed or got it wrong, and what did you learn from it?
5. What's the moment that defines why you do the work you do — the experience that made your mission clear?

After I share all 5 stories, do the following for each one:

**Story Title:** A punchy 4-6 word title I can use to reference it quickly
**The Setup:** 2 sentences — the context and the stakes
**The Turn:** What I did, decided, or discovered (the interesting part)
**The Result:** Concrete outcome — numbers, promotions, transformations, lessons
**Best Used For:** Interview answers / LinkedIn content / keynote openers / networking conversations
**One-Line Version:** The story in a single sentence — for moments when I have 30 seconds

Save the complete story bank as my reference library.
```

---

## How to use your story bank

- **Job interviews:** Match the story to the competency being tested. Your story bank covers leadership, resilience, risk, failure, and mission.
- **LinkedIn content:** Each story is a post waiting to happen. The "One-Line Version" is your hook.
- **Networking:** The one-line version is your answer to "tell me about yourself" that actually makes people curious.
- **Keynotes and speaking:** The "Why I do this" story is almost always your opening.

**Re-run this skill every 12-18 months.** Your story bank should grow with your career.
