# profile-rewrite
**Rebuild your LinkedIn profile so it attracts the right opportunities — instead of just documenting your past.**

Most LinkedIn profiles read like a resume. A resume is a record of where you've been. Your LinkedIn profile should make the right people want to reach out to where you're going.

---

## The Prompt

```
Read my voice-builder and audience-builder profiles if they exist in this project.

I want to rewrite my LinkedIn profile so it works as a magnet — attracting the right opportunities, conversations, and people instead of just listing my history.

Start by asking me these questions one at a time:

1. Paste your current LinkedIn headline.
2. Paste your current About section.
3. What opportunities do you actually want to attract? (Types of roles, speaking invitations, consulting work, board seats, partnerships — be specific)
4. What do people always ask you about or come to you for? What are you the "go-to" person for in your world?
5. What's your proudest career achievement in the last 3 years — and what made it possible?
6. What do you want someone to feel 30 seconds after landing on your profile?

After I answer, rewrite the following sections:

**HEADLINE (220 characters max)**
Not your job title. The specific transformation you create or the specific person you help. Make someone want to click.

**ABOUT SECTION (under 2,000 characters)**
- Open with a hook — a belief, a bold statement, or a moment (not "I am a...")
- 2-3 paragraphs that tell your professional story through the lens of who you help and how
- What you're currently focused on / open to
- A clear, low-friction CTA at the end (DM, email, or calendar link)

**FEATURED SECTION suggestions**
3 things worth pinning based on what I've shared — lead magnet, speaking reel, key article, case study, etc.

**EXPERIENCE section framing (for my most recent 2 roles)**
Reframe bullet points from "responsibilities" to "outcomes" — what changed because I was there.

Write everything in my voice. No buzzwords. No "passionate about" or "results-driven" or "thought leader."
```

---

## Pro tips after your rewrite

- **Update your headline first.** It appears everywhere — search results, comments, connection requests.
- **Your About section gets read when someone is already interested.** The headline is what makes them click.
- **Revisit every 6 months** or whenever your focus shifts.
- **Add your rewritten profile to this Claude Project** so future skills can reference your positioning.
