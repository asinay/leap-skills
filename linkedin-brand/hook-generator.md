# hook-generator
**The first line is everything. This skill writes 10 hook variations for any post so you never open with something forgettable.**

LinkedIn truncates your post after 2-3 lines. If your opening doesn't earn the "see more" click, the rest of the post doesn't exist. This skill treats the hook as a craft problem and solves it.

---

## The Prompt

```
Read my voice-builder profile if it exists in this project.

I need strong opening lines for a LinkedIn post. The topic is: [paste your topic or rough post idea here]

Generate 10 hook variations using these 10 patterns — one per pattern:

1. **The Counterintuitive Truth** — State the opposite of what people expect to hear
2. **The Specific Number** — A data point, timeframe, or outcome that earns credibility
3. **The Confession** — Something you got wrong, struggled with, or were embarrassed by
4. **The Observation** — Something you've noticed that others haven't named yet
5. **The Direct Address** — Speak directly to the exact person this is for
6. **The Story Opener** — Drop into a specific moment in time (year, place, situation)
7. **The Strong Opinion** — A clear position that some people will disagree with
8. **The Reframe** — Take a commonly held belief and flip it
9. **The Stakes** — Establish what's at risk if the reader keeps doing what they're doing
10. **The Curiosity Gap** — Tease the insight without giving it away yet

Rules for every hook:
- Under 12 words when possible; 15 maximum
- No question hooks (they underperform on LinkedIn)
- No "I'm excited to share..." or "Today I want to talk about..."
- No "In today's world..." or "As a [title]..."
- Must earn the second line — the reader should want to know what comes next

After the 10 variations, flag your top 3 with ⭐ and explain in one sentence why each one works.
```

---

## How to pick your hook

Ask yourself:
- Would I stop scrolling for this?
- Does it make a specific person feel seen or challenged?
- Does it create a question in the reader's mind that the post will answer?

If the answer to all three is yes — use it.

**Run this skill before you write any post**, not after. The hook shapes the whole piece.
